Run a Discovery: step by step

Before you start designing a service, learn about your users and the wider context so that you can build the right thing.

  1. Step 1 Set a goal

    A discovery should help you decide whether or not to start prototyping in an Alpha phase. Starting with a clear goal will keep your discovery to the right sized scope and help you decide when you are done.

    1. Choose a goal for your discovery

  2. Step 2 Get funding

    1. Check if you need spend controls approval
    2. Check what roles you'll need in your team
    3. Understand how long a Discovery might take

    Once you've done these things, you'll need to submit your business case through your department's processes.

  3. Step 3 Plan the research activities

    1. Plan the user research
    2. Plan the technology research
    3. Plan the business and policy analysisMissing content

  4. Step 4 Learn about your users needs and context

      For this part of the project, you'll need a dedicated and experienced user researcher.

    1. Learn about your users and their needs

  5. and Learn about the technology landscape

    For this part of the project, you'll need a technical architect.

    1. How to make decisions about technology
    2. What a technical architect does in discovery

  6. and Understand the policy and legal context

    You'll need a business analyst and a subject matter expert for this part of the discovery.

    1. How to analyse the political and legal context you're operating inMissing content

  7. Step 5 Decide if you've learnt enough

    1. How you know a discovery is finished
    2. Wrap up and share your findings

  8. Step 6 Run Alpha projects as needed

    The next step might be to run an Alpha, multiple Alphas, do more Discovery, or do nothing.
